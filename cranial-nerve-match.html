Clinical Connection

SLPs routinely observe cranial nerve function during oral mechanism exams, motor speech assessment, voice assessment, and swallowing evaluation. The goal is not to diagnose nerve damage, but to recognize patterns that may affect speech, voice, resonance, hearing, oral movement, or swallowing safety.
