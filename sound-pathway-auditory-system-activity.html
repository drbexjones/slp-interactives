How Sound Moves Through the Auditory System

Arrange the pathway of sound from the environment to the brain. This activity focuses on the major anatomical structures students need to recognize and explain in an SLP anatomy course.

Task: Drag each structure into the correct step of the auditory pathway. You can also click a term, then click a numbered slot to place it.

Tip: Think outer ear → middle ear → inner ear → nerve pathway → auditory cortex.

Structure Bank

Sound Pathway

Correct Pathway Explanation

  1. Pinna/Auricle: Collects sound waves from the environment.
  2. External Auditory Canal: Funnels sound toward the tympanic membrane.
  3. Tympanic Membrane: Vibrates in response to sound pressure changes.
  4. Ossicles: The malleus, incus, and stapes amplify and transfer vibration through the middle ear.
  5. Oval Window: Receives vibration from the stapes and transfers mechanical energy into the cochlea.
  6. Cochlea: Converts mechanical vibration into fluid movement and stimulates sensory hair cells.
  7. Organ of Corti/Hair Cells: Transduces mechanical movement into neural signals.
  8. Auditory Nerve/CN VIII: Carries auditory information toward the brainstem.
  9. Brainstem Auditory Pathway: Relays and processes auditory information through subcortical structures.
  10. Primary Auditory Cortex: Interprets sound at the cortical level.
