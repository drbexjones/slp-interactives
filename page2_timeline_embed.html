Semester 1
Fall  ·  Building Your Foundation
⌄
🏫
On-Campus Residency: Education/Pediatric OCR 1
Your first immersive clinical experience happens right here on the OBU campus. Over five intensive days, you'll follow a single pediatric client case from referral through IEP development, practicing real clinical skills in a supported environment alongside your cohort and faculty.
💻
Virtual Practicum (SLP 5212)
Running alongside your coursework for the full semester, the Virtual Practicum uses Simucase to give you supervised clinical hours before you enter an external placement. Hours completed here count toward your 375-hour requirement.
Semester 2
Spring  ·  Entering the School Setting
⌄
🎒
Education/Pediatric Externship (SLP 6002)
Your first real-world externship places you in an educational setting — typically a school — where you'll work with children under the supervision of a qualified SLP clinical educator. You'll apply the skills from Semester 1 with real clients, real data, and real IEPs. This placement runs for the full semester, a minimum of three days per week.
Semester 3
Summer  ·  Expanding Your Clinical Scope
⌄
🏥
On-Campus Residency: Medical/Adult OCR 2
Your second OCR brings you back to campus for another intensive week, this time focused on adult and medical populations — dysphagia, neurogenic disorders, voice, and more. You'll work through a new case with new faculty and peers, deepening your clinical reasoning across a different scope of practice.
🩺
Medical/Adult Externship (SLP 6212)
This externship places you in a medical or clinical setting — hospitals, rehabilitation centers, outpatient clinics, or skilled nursing facilities — where you'll work with adult populations under clinical supervision. This runs for the full semester at a minimum of three days per week.
Semester 4
Fall  ·  Advanced Clinical Practice
⌄
⭐
Advanced Clinical Externship (SLP 6402)
Your final externship is an advanced placement — often a telepractice setting or specialized clinical context — where you'll bring together everything you've learned. By this point you're functioning with greater independence and demonstrating readiness for entry-level professional practice. This is the capstone of your clinical education.
